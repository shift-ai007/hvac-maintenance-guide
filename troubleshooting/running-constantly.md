# Troubleshooting: AC Running Constantly

Your system never seems to cycle off. Here's how to diagnose it.

## Is It Actually a Problem?

On the hottest South Florida days (95F+), it's normal for your AC to run 80-90% of the time. The real concern is if it runs nonstop AND can't reach your set temperature.

**Normal:** System runs most of the day but maintains set temperature (within 2F).
**Problem:** System runs continuously and temperature keeps climbing or never reaches the setpoint.

## Causes and Fixes

### 1. Thermostat Set Too Low
Setting the thermostat to 68F when it's 97F outside asks your system to achieve a 29F differential. Most residential systems can only manage 15-20F.

**Fix:** Set thermostat to 76-78F. If the system maintains that, it's working correctly.

### 2. Dirty Air Filter
Restricted airflow reduces cooling capacity. The system runs longer trying to compensate.

**Fix:** Replace the filter.

### 3. Dirty Condenser Coils
Dirt and debris on the outdoor coils prevent heat dissipation.

**Fix:** Rinse coils with a garden hose (top to bottom, system OFF).

### 4. Low Refrigerant
Reduced refrigerant means reduced cooling capacity. The system works harder to move less heat.

**Signs:** Warm air from vents, ice on refrigerant lines, hissing sound near indoor unit.

**Fix:** Call a professional. Refrigerant leaks need repair, not just a recharge.

### 5. Undersized System
If the system has always struggled on hot days, it may be too small for your home.

**Signs:** System was installed by a previous owner, no Manual J calculation was done, home has been modified (additions, removed shade trees, new windows).

**Fix:** Get a professional load calculation.

### 6. Duct Leaks
Cooled air escaping into the attic or crawlspace means your system is cooling spaces you don't live in.

**Test:** On a hot day, feel for air movement around duct joints in the attic. You can also hold a lit incense stick near joints — smoke movement indicates leaks.

**Fix:** Professional duct sealing ($800-1,500, typically pays for itself in 1-2 years).

### 7. Poor Insulation
Heat flooding in through the attic, walls, or windows faster than the AC can remove it.

**Fix:** Add attic insulation, install window film or solar screens, seal air leaks.
