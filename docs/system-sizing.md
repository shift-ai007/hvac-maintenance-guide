# AC System Sizing for South Florida Homes

Getting the right size AC system is critical in South Florida. Oversized and undersized systems both waste energy and reduce comfort.

## Why Sizing Matters

**Oversized system problems:**
- Cools too fast, doesn't run long enough to dehumidify
- Short cycling wears out the compressor faster
- House feels cold but clammy
- Higher purchase cost for no benefit

**Undersized system problems:**
- Runs constantly and never reaches set temperature
- Excessive energy bills
- Premature wear on all components
- Poor comfort during peak summer heat

## General Sizing Guidelines

These are rough estimates for South Florida homes with standard insulation. Actual requirements depend on many factors.

| Home Size (sqft) | Cooling Capacity | System Size |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 800-1,000 | 18,000-24,000 BTU | 1.5-2 tons |
| 1,000-1,300 | 24,000-30,000 BTU | 2-2.5 tons |
| 1,300-1,600 | 30,000-36,000 BTU | 2.5-3 tons |
| 1,600-2,000 | 36,000-42,000 BTU | 3-3.5 tons |
| 2,000-2,400 | 42,000-48,000 BTU | 3.5-4 tons |
| 2,400-3,000 | 48,000-60,000 BTU | 4-5 tons |

*1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hour of cooling capacity*

## Factors That Increase Sizing Needs

- West or south-facing large windows
- Minimal tree shade
- Older single-pane windows
- Vaulted ceilings
- Below-code insulation
- Kitchen or laundry on upper floor
- More than 4 occupants
- Frequent door opening (home office with clients)

## Factors That Decrease Sizing Needs

- Heavy tree shade on roof and walls
- Impact windows with Low-E coating
- R-38+ attic insulation
- Light-colored roof
- Radiant barrier in attic
- Single-story with slab foundation

## Manual J Load Calculation

The only accurate way to size an AC system is a Manual J load calculation. This factors in:

- Square footage and ceiling height
- Window area, type, and orientation
- Insulation R-values (walls, attic, floor)
- Number of occupants
- Appliance heat generation
- Infiltration rate (air leakage)
- Duct location and condition
- Local climate data (South Florida = Zone 1)

**Cost:** $100-300 from an HVAC contractor or energy auditor.

**Important:** Many contractors skip Manual J and size by "rules of thumb" (e.g., 1 ton per 500 sqft). This is unreliable — two 2,000 sqft homes can need very different systems depending on construction and orientation.

## When to Replace vs. Repair

| Factor | Lean Repair | Lean Replace |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| System age | Under 10 years | Over 12-15 years |
| Repair cost | Under 50% of new system | Over 50% of new system |
| Current SEER | 14+ SEER | Under 12 SEER |
| Refrigerant | R-410A | R-22 (phased out) |
| Repair frequency | First major repair | Third+ repair in 2 years |
| Comfort | Generally good | Persistent humidity/temp issues |

**R-22 systems:** If your system uses R-22 refrigerant (common in systems installed before 2010), replacement is strongly recommended. R-22 production ended in 2020, making it expensive ($100-200/lb) and increasingly scarce.

---

*Always get a Manual J calculation before purchasing a new system. A properly sized system costs less to operate and lasts longer than an oversized one.*
