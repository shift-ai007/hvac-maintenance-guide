# Heat Pump Guide for South Florida Homeowners

## Can a Heat Pump Work in South Florida?

Yes — and for many homes, a modern heat pump is actually more efficient than a standard air conditioner. While cold-climate heat pumps get most of the press, the technology has become one of the best options for subtropical climates too. The key is choosing a unit designed for our actual conditions: 8+ months of cooling with high humidity, not Minnesota winters.

## Heat Pump vs Standard AC: What's Different

A standard air conditioner moves heat from inside your home to the outside. A heat pump does the same for cooling, but reverses the cycle for heating — pulling ambient heat from outdoor air into your home. Because South Florida rarely drops below 40°F, there's always enough heat in the outside air for efficient extraction.

**Two specs that matter:**
- **SEER2** — Cooling efficiency (same as standard ACs). Aim for ≥18.
- **HSPF2** — Heating efficiency (heat pumps only). Secondary concern in Florida.

The reversing valve is the one extra component. Modern valves are reliable, but should be tested during annual maintenance.

## Energy Savings

Homeowners who switch from a 10–12 SEER standard AC to a high-SEER heat pump typically see:

| Metric | Savings |
|--------|---------|
| Summer cooling | 20–35% reduction |
| Winter heating | $80–200/year vs electric strip heat |
| Payback period | 3–5 years on the $800–1,500 premium |

Combined federal tax credits (30%, up to $2,000) and utility rebates (FPL $300–600, others $200–500) can cover 25–40% of the premium.

## Which Configuration?

- **Ducted (central)** — Replaces existing central AC. Best with well-sealed ducts.
- **Ductless mini-split** — No ducts needed. Ideal for additions, garages, or zoned comfort.
- **Hybrid** — Central for main areas, ductless for a bonus room or garage.

## What to Look For

1. **SEER2 ≥ 18** — Best payback for our climate
2. **Variable-speed compressor** — Better humidity removal
3. **Corrosion-resistant coil** — Coastal warranty recommended
4. **Skip cold-climate features** — Not needed here

## Maintenance

Same as standard AC — coil cleaning, filter changes, refrigerant checks, twice-yearly. Add reversing valve testing to the checklist.
