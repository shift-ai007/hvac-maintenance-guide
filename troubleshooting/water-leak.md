# Troubleshooting: Water Leaking Inside

Water around your indoor AC unit or water stains on the ceiling. This is the #1 AC problem in South Florida.

## Step 1: Turn Off the System
Before investigating, set the thermostat to OFF. This prevents additional water from being produced while you diagnose.

## Step 2: Check the Condensate Drain Line

**Location:** Follow the PVC pipe from your indoor unit to where it exits the house (usually near the outdoor unit or through a wall).

**Test:** Pour water into the drain pan or access point. Does it flow out the other end?

- **If water flows freely:** The drain isn't clogged. Skip to Step 4.
- **If water doesn't flow or flows slowly:** Clog found. Continue to Step 3.

## Step 3: Clear the Clog

**Method 1 — Vinegar flush:**
1. Locate the drain line access point (T-shaped PVC fitting near indoor unit)
2. Pour 1 cup of white vinegar into the opening
3. Wait 30 minutes
4. Flush with 1 cup of warm water
5. Check the drain outlet — water should flow out

**Method 2 — Wet/dry vacuum:**
1. Connect the vacuum hose to the drain line outlet (outside)
2. Seal the connection with a rag or tape
3. Run the vacuum for 30-60 seconds
4. Check the vacuum canister for debris

**Method 3 — Manual clearing:**
1. Use a thin, flexible brush or pipe cleaner
2. Insert through the access point
3. Gently push through the clog
4. Flush with water after

**If none of these work → Call a pro.** The clog may be deep in the line or the line itself may be damaged.

## Step 4: Check the Drain Pan

The drain pan sits under the evaporator coil and catches condensation.

- Is the pan cracked or rusted through?
- Is the pan tilted (not draining toward the drain line)?
- Is there standing water even though the drain line is clear?

**If pan is damaged:** Needs replacement → Call a pro.

## Step 5: Check for Ice Melt

If the indoor unit was iced over (frozen evaporator coil), the leak may be from melting ice — not a drain problem.

**Signs of ice melt vs. drain clog:**
- Ice melt: Large volume of water, possibly dripping from unusual spots
- Drain clog: Steady drip from the drain pan area

**If ice was the cause:** See [AC Not Cooling](not-cooling.md) for freeze troubleshooting.

## Step 6: Check the Air Handler Cabinet

- Are there water stains or rust on the air handler cabinet?
- Is the secondary drain line (backup drain, usually on the ceiling) dripping? This means the primary drain failed.

**If secondary drain is active:** The primary drain is completely blocked and needs professional clearing.

## Prevention

To avoid future water leaks:
- Flush drain line with vinegar monthly during summer
- Keep air filter clean (dirty filter → frozen coil → water)
- Schedule annual maintenance that includes drain line cleaning
- Consider installing a safety float switch if you don't have one (shuts off AC if drain backs up)

## Emergency: Active Water Damage

If water is actively damaging your ceiling or floors:
1. Turn off AC immediately
2. Place towels or a bucket to catch water
3. If water is near electrical equipment, turn off the breaker
4. Call an HVAC professional for same-day service
5. Document damage with photos for insurance
