# Common AC Problems in South Florida

These are the issues we see most frequently in South Florida homes, ranked by how often they occur.

## 1. Clogged Condensate Drain Line

**Frequency:** Most common AC problem in South Florida, by far.

**Symptoms:**
- Water leaking from indoor air handler
- Water stains on ceiling near AC unit
- Musty smell from vents
- System shuts off unexpectedly (float switch triggered)

**Cause:** South Florida's humidity means your AC removes 5-20 gallons of water per day. This water drains through a PVC pipe. Algae, mold, and debris clog this line — especially in summer when the system runs constantly.

**DIY Fix:**
1. Locate the drain line (usually a PVC pipe near the outdoor unit)
2. Pour 1 cup of white vinegar into the drain line access point
3. Wait 30 minutes
4. Flush with warm water
5. Repeat monthly during summer

**When to call a pro:** If the line is completely blocked and vinegar doesn't clear it. Technicians use nitrogen or a wet/dry vac to clear stubborn clogs.

## 2. Dirty Air Filter

**Frequency:** Second most common, and the easiest to prevent.

**Symptoms:**
- Reduced airflow from vents
- System runs longer than usual
- Higher electric bills
- Ice forming on evaporator coil
- Uneven cooling between rooms

**Cause:** Filters in South Florida homes clog faster due to higher particulate levels (pollen, dust, pet dander, construction dust). A clogged filter restricts airflow, forcing the system to work harder.

**Fix:**
- 1" filters: Replace every 30 days
- 4" filters: Replace every 60-90 days
- Reusable filters: Clean monthly, replace annually

**Tip:** Set a phone reminder. A $5 filter change prevents $500+ repairs.

## 3. Refrigerant Leak

**Symptoms:**
- System blows warm air
- Ice on refrigerant lines or evaporator coil
- Hissing or bubbling sound near indoor unit
- Electric bills increasing without explanation
- System runs constantly without reaching set temperature

**Cause:** Vibration, corrosion (especially near the coast), or manufacturing defects create small leaks in refrigerant lines or coils. South Florida's salt air accelerates corrosion.

**Important:** Refrigerant doesn't "run out" — if levels are low, there's a leak. Simply adding refrigerant without fixing the leak wastes money.

**Always call a pro:** EPA regulations require certified technicians to handle refrigerants. Never attempt this yourself.

## 4. Capacitor Failure

**Symptoms:**
- System hums but doesn't start
- Fan doesn't spin (or spins slowly)
- System starts then immediately shuts off
- Clicking sounds from outdoor unit

**Cause:** Capacitors store and release electrical energy to start the compressor and fan motors. South Florida's heat accelerates capacitor wear — they're essentially electrolytic cans that dry out faster in high temperatures.

**Lifespan:** 5-10 years in South Florida (vs 15-20 years in cooler climates).

**Call a pro:** Capacitors store dangerous electrical charge even when the system is off. Not a safe DIY repair.

## 5. Frozen Evaporator Coil

**Symptoms:**
- Warm air from vents
- Visible ice on indoor refrigerant lines
- Water leaking when ice melts
- System runs but doesn't cool

**Common causes in South Florida:**
1. Dirty air filter (restricts airflow over the coil)
2. Low refrigerant (coil gets too cold)
3. Blower motor failure (no air moving over coil)
4. Dirty evaporator coil (insulates the coil from airflow)
5. Running AC below 68F in high humidity (coil drops below freezing)

**Emergency fix:**
1. Turn system OFF at thermostat
2. Set fan to ON (this blows warm air over the coil to defrost it)
3. Wait 2-4 hours for ice to fully melt
4. Check and replace air filter
5. Turn system back to COOL

If it freezes again, call a professional — you likely have a refrigerant leak or blower issue.

## 6. Thermostat Problems

**Symptoms:**
- System doesn't respond to temperature changes
- Room temperature doesn't match thermostat reading
- System short-cycles (turns on/off rapidly)
- Blank thermostat display

**Common causes:**
- Dead batteries (most common)
- Thermostat in direct sunlight (reads artificially high)
- Loose wiring connections
- Thermostat located near heat source (lamp, oven, window)

**DIY fixes:**
- Replace batteries
- Relocate thermostat away from direct sunlight and heat sources
- Ensure thermostat is level and securely mounted
- Clean dust from inside the thermostat housing

## 7. Compressor Failure

**Symptoms:**
- Outdoor unit runs but doesn't cool
- Loud grinding or clunking from outdoor unit
- Circuit breaker trips when AC starts
- System doesn't start at all

**Cause:** Compressors fail from age, electrical problems, refrigerant issues, or contaminated oil. In South Florida, the constant workload accelerates wear.

**Cost reality:** Compressor replacement typically costs $1,500-3,000+ for parts and labor. If your system is 10+ years old, replacing the entire system is often more cost-effective.

**Always call a pro.** Compressor work requires specialized tools and EPA certification.

*These problems cover about 80% of service calls in South Florida. For other issues, consult a licensed HVAC technician.*
