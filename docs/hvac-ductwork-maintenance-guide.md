# HVAC Ductwork Maintenance for South Florida Homes

Your ductwork is the circulatory system of your home's air conditioning. While filters and coils get most of the attention, the ducts running through your attic, crawlspace, and walls determine whether that cool air actually reaches your living spaces efficiently. In South Florida's extreme heat and humidity, duct problems waste more energy and cause more comfort issues than almost any other AC component.

## Why Ductwork Matters in South Florida

A typical South Florida home loses 20-30% of its conditioned air through duct leaks, poor insulation, and disconnected sections — before the air ever reaches a vent. In a climate where your AC runs 8+ months a year, that waste translates to hundreds of dollars in extra electricity bills and significant strain on your equipment.

Beyond energy waste, South Florida's unique environment creates duct problems you won't see in drier climates:

- **Condensation inside ducts** — Humid attic air meeting cold duct surfaces creates moisture that feeds mold growth
- **Insulation degradation** — The foam and fiberglass wrapping on attic ducts deteriorates faster in our heat and humidity
- **Pest intrusion** — Rats, squirrels, and insects love cool, dark ducts during Florida summers
- **Leaks from settling** — Our sandy soil causes slab shifts that can separate duct joints over time

## Signs Your Ductwork Needs Attention

Your home gives clear signals when ducts are failing. Watch for these signs:

| Symptom | Likely Duct Problem |
|---------|---------------------|
| Uneven temperatures room-to-room | Leaks or disconnections in branch runs |
| Higher than normal electric bills | Overall leakage reducing system efficiency |
| Musty odors from vents | Mold or mildew growing inside ducts |
| Visible dust around vents | Leaking joints at register boots |
| Rooms feel stuffy or humid | Insufficient return air duct capacity |
| Whistling from vents | Undersized or restricted ducts |

## DIY Duct Inspection Checklist

You can inspect much of your ductwork without special tools. Here's what to check:

### Visual Inspection (Accessible Ducts in Attic or Crawlspace)

1. **Look for disconnected sections** — Flex duct that has pulled loose from a metal collar is the most common leak in South Florida homes. The connection may look fine from outside but be completely separated inside the plastic wrap.

2. **Check insulation condition** — Duct wrap insulation should be at least R-6 (1.5 inches thick) in Florida attics. Look for bare spots, water stains, or insulation that has fallen off entirely. Foam insulation boards should be tightly sealed at all seams with foil tape — not duct tape, which degrades in weeks.

3. **Inspect flex duct for kinks** — A crushed or sharply bent flex duct can reduce airflow by 50% or more. Flex duct should run in gentle curves, not tight 90-degree bends.

4. **Check for rodent damage** — Look for chewed insulation, droppings near duct joints, or actual holes in the duct wall. Rats and squirrels can destroy a duct system in a single season.

5. **Verify support straps** — Flex duct should be supported every 4-6 feet with straps or hangers. Sagging ducts collect condensation in the low spots and restrict airflow.

### Performance Checks

- **The tissue test** — Hold a tissue near each supply vent while the AC runs. It should blow firmly. Then hold it near the return grille — it should be sucked inward. Weak pressure at either end means duct problems.

- **Temperature check** — Use an instant-read thermometer at the supply vent closest to the air handler and the farthest one. More than 5 degrees difference suggests significant duct leakage or restriction.

## Condensation and Mold Prevention

Condensation inside ducts is South Florida's most persistent ductwork problem. Here's how to prevent it:

- **Seal all duct joints with mastic** — Not duct tape. Mastic is a brush-on paste that hardens into a permanent, airtight seal. It's cheap, easy to apply, and available at any hardware store.

- **Ensure proper insulation thickness** — Florida Building Code requires R-8 duct insulation in attics (about 2 inches of fiberglass wrap or 1 inch of closed-cell foam board). If your insulation is thinner, consider adding a second layer.

- **Fix attic ventilation issues** — A hot, humid attic makes condensation worse. Make sure soffit vents aren't blocked by insulation, and that attic fans (if installed) are working properly.

- **Check the condensate drain** — A clogged drain that backs up can spill water directly into ductwork through the air handler. See our [condensate drain maintenance guide](docs/condensate-drain-maintenance.md) for prevention tips.

## Duct Leak Detection Methods

### The Smoke Test

On a still day (rare in Florida, but possible early morning), hold a lit incense stick near duct joints while the AC fan runs. If the smoke gets sucked into or blown out of the joint, you have a leak.

### The Pressure Test (For the Ambitious DIYer)

Close all supply vents in the house, remove the filter from the return grille, and run the fan only (no cooling). Place your hand over the return opening — you should feel strong suction. Weak suction means the overall duct system has significant leakage.

### Professional Testing (When to Call)

If your electric bills are high and you've ruled out other causes, a professional duct leakage test using a duct blaster is worth the investment. In Florida, many utility companies subsidize these tests — check with your provider.

## Sealing Leaks: DIY vs Professional

### DIY-Repairable Leaks

- Small holes (up to 2 inches) in accessible flex duct
- Loose connections you can reach and wrap with foil tape and mastic
- Minor insulation gaps (add a wrap of foil-faced insulation)

### Call a Professional For

- Leaks in ducts buried in walls or slabs
- Major rodent damage requiring duct replacement
- Entire system leakage exceeding 20% (measured by a duct blaster)
- Mold contamination inside ducts requiring remediation
- Undersized return ducts causing airflow problems

| Frequency | Task |
|-----------|------|
| **Monthly** | Check visible duct insulation for damage after storms |
| **Quarterly** | Inspect accessible duct joints for leaks during filter changes |
| **Every 6 months** | Test vent airflow (tissue test) and check for musty odors |
| **Annually** | Professional HVAC maintenance visit that includes duct inspection — schedule with [AC maintenance services](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-maintenance/) |
| **After hurricanes** | Full attic duct inspection for water damage, torn insulation, and pest intrusion |

## When Duct Replacement Makes Sense

Duct systems typically last 15-20 years in South Florida's climate. Consider replacement if:

- Your duct insulation is the original gray fiberglass wrap (pre-2000s)
- You've had multiple rodent or pest intrusions
- You've patched leaks more than 3-4 times in the same area
- You're replacing your AC system and the ducts don't match the new unit's airflow requirements

New properly sealed and insulated ductwork typically pays for itself within 3-5 years through energy savings alone, especially with duct systems located in unconditioned attics.

## Bottom Line

Your ducts are the most overlooked part of your home's AC system, yet they're responsible for moving every cubic foot of conditioned air to where it needs to go. A few hours of annual duct inspection and maintenance can prevent the most common comfort complaints and keep your energy bills in check — no matter what a South Florida summer throws at your home.
