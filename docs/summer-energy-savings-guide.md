# Summer Energy Savings Guide for South Florida Homeowners

> A practical guide to reducing AC energy consumption during the hottest months in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties.

## Why Summer Bills Spike

In South Florida, summer cooling costs typically account for 50-60% of a home's total electric bill. The combination of high outdoor temperatures (mid-90s F), extreme humidity (70%+), and long cooling seasons creates conditions where even well-maintained systems work near their maximum capacity.

The good news is that most homes can save 20-40% on summer cooling costs through a combination of professional maintenance and behavioral changes.

## Quick Wins (Under $50)

| Fix | Cost | Est. Savings |

| Install programmable thermostat | $30-50 | 10-15% |

## Professional Interventions ($100-500)

### Duct Leakage Test and Sealing
Leaky ducts are the single largest energy waste in Florida homes. A blower door test identifies leaks, and mastic sealing can reduce AC runtime by 15-25%. Cost: $150-300.

### Refrigerant Charge Correction
Low refrigerant forces the compressor to run longer. Symptoms include warm air from vents, ice on the evaporator coil, and running 16+ hours per day. A professional charge check costs $80-150.

### Condenser and Evaporator Coil Cleaning
Professional coil cleaning with chemical agents removes accumulated grime that DIY hosing cannot touch. Restores 10-15% efficiency. Cost: $150-200.

## When to Call a Pro

If your AC runs 16+ hours per day despite clean filters and proper thermostat settings, or if you notice ice forming on refrigerant lines, it is time for a professional diagnosis.

Professional AC maintenance services can identify the root cause and restore efficiency before the system fails on the hottest day of the year.
