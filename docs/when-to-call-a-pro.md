# DIY vs. Professional AC Repairs

Knowing what you can handle yourself and what requires a licensed technician saves money and prevents costly mistakes.

## Safe to DIY

These tasks require no special tools or training:

| Task | Difficulty | Time | Frequency |
|------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Replace air filter | Easy | 2 min | Monthly |
| Clear debris around condenser | Easy | 10 min | Monthly |
| Flush drain line with vinegar | Easy | 5 min | Monthly |
| Replace thermostat batteries | Easy | 2 min | Annually |
| Clean supply/return vents | Easy | 15 min | Quarterly |
| Rinse condenser coils (hose) | Easy | 15 min | Quarterly |
| Check refrigerant line insulation | Easy | 5 min | Quarterly |
| Program thermostat schedule | Easy | 10 min | Once |

## Call a Professional

These require specialized tools, EPA certification, or risk personal injury:

| Issue | Why Professional | Typical Cost |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Refrigerant recharge | EPA certification required | $150-500 |
| Electrical repairs | Shock/fire hazard, permitting | $100-400 |
| Compressor replacement | Heavy, specialized tools | $1,500-3,000 |
| Capacitor replacement | Stored electrical charge danger | $150-300 |
| Blower motor replacement | Wiring, calibration needed | $300-700 |
| Evaporator coil replacement | Refrigerant handling, brazing | $800-2,000 |
| Duct repair/sealing | Access difficulty, testing | $800-2,000 |
| Full system replacement | Permitting, sizing, refrigerant | $5,000-12,000 |
| Annual tune-up | Comprehensive diagnostics | $80-150 |

## Emergency Situations — Call Immediately

- **Burning smell** from vents or outdoor unit — turn off system at breaker, call HVAC and possibly fire department
- **Electrical sparking** at disconnect or indoor unit
- **Hissing sound + oil stains** near refrigerant lines (refrigerant leak)
- **Standing water** around indoor air handler that won't stop
- **System trips breaker repeatedly** after reset

## How to Choose a Contractor in Florida

1. **Verify license** at [myfloridalicense.com](https://www.myfloridalicense.com/) — look for active CAC license
2. **Check insurance** — ask for certificate of liability and workers' comp
3. **Get multiple quotes** for non-emergency work (3 quotes minimum for replacements)
4. **Ask about permits** — reputable contractors pull permits for replacements and major repairs
5. **Avoid "refrigerant top-off" services** — if refrigerant is low, there's a leak that needs repair

## Red Flags

- No Florida license number on vehicle, card, or invoice
- Pressure to decide immediately on a system replacement
- Refrigerant "top-off" without leak testing
- Quotes significantly below market rate (may be unlicensed)
- Won't pull required permits
- Cash-only, no written invoice

---

*Always verify contractor licensing before authorizing work. Florida law requires HVAC contractors to hold an active CAC license for any work involving refrigerant circuits, electrical connections, or gas lines.*
