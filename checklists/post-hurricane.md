# Post-Hurricane HVAC Restart Checklist

Follow this sequence after any hurricane or tropical storm. Do NOT skip steps.

## Safety First
- [ ] Confirm power is restored and stable to your area
- [ ] Check for downed power lines near outdoor unit
- [ ] Check for gas leaks if you have a gas furnace (smell test)
- [ ] Ensure no standing water around electrical equipment

## Visual Inspection (Outdoor)
- [ ] Walk around the condenser unit — note any visible damage
- [ ] Check for debris on top of or inside the unit
- [ ] Inspect refrigerant lines for bends, breaks, or missing insulation
- [ ] Check electrical disconnect box for water intrusion
- [ ] Check condenser unit — was it submerged? Even partially? (If YES → STOP, call a pro)

## Clear & Clean
- [ ] Remove all debris from around the unit (branches, leaves, fencing)
- [ ] Remove debris from the top of the unit
- [ ] Gently rinse condenser coils with garden hose
- [ ] Clear condensate drain line

## Indoor Check
- [ ] Inspect air handler area for water intrusion
- [ ] Check air filter — replace if wet or damaged
- [ ] Check for water stains on ceiling near AC unit
- [ ] Verify condensate drain pan is not overflowing

## Restart (only if no flooding or visible damage)
- [ ] Turn ON breaker at main panel
- [ ] Turn ON electrical disconnect at outdoor unit
- [ ] WAIT 30 MINUTES (pressure equalization)
- [ ] Set thermostat to COOL, 78F
- [ ] Listen for normal startup sounds
- [ ] Confirm cool air from vents within 5 minutes
- [ ] Monitor for 30 minutes — check for unusual noises or smells

## Warning Signs — Call a Professional
- [ ] Grinding or screeching sounds
- [ ] Burning smell from any component
- [ ] Unit runs but blows warm air
- [ ] Breaker trips when system starts
- [ ] Water leaking from indoor unit
- [ ] Any component was submerged in floodwater
