# Cutting Your Cooling Costs in South Florida

The average South Florida household spends $200-400/month on electricity during summer, with AC accounting for 50-70% of that bill. Here's how to reduce it.

## Thermostat Settings

The single biggest factor in your electric bill.

- **Set to 78F when home.** Every degree below 78F increases energy use by 3-5%.
- **Set to 82-85F when away.** Don't turn it off completely — restarting a hot house costs more than maintaining a slightly warm one.
- **Use a programmable thermostat.** Set it to cool down 30 minutes before you arrive home.
- **Fan mode: AUTO, not ON.** "ON" runs the blower 24/7, adding $30-50/month and reducing dehumidification.

## Air Filter Discipline

A dirty filter increases energy consumption by 5-15%.

- Change 1" filters monthly during May-October
- Change quarterly during November-April
- Write the change date on the filter with a marker
- Buy filters in bulk — a 12-pack of MERV 8 filters costs $40-60 and lasts a year

## Seal Your Envelope

Air leaks force your AC to work harder.

**Check these areas:**
- Windows and doors (use weatherstripping)
- Electrical outlets on exterior walls (use foam gaskets)
- Recessed lights in ceilinged rooms below the attic
- Attic hatch (add insulation and weatherstripping)
- Plumbing and electrical penetrations through walls

**Duct leaks:** The average South Florida home loses 20-30% of cooled air through duct leaks. Professional duct sealing (aerosol-based or mastic) typically costs $800-1,500 and pays for itself within 1-2 cooling seasons.

## Insulation

Florida Building Code minimums are often insufficient for optimal efficiency.

| Area | Minimum (Code) | Recommended |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| Attic | R-30 | R-38 to R-49 |
| Walls | R-13 | R-13 to R-15 |
| Crawlspace | R-13 | R-19 |

Adding attic insulation from R-30 to R-49 typically costs $1,000-2,000 and saves 10-15% on cooling costs.

## Window Strategies

South Florida sun is intense. Windows are the biggest source of heat gain.

- **Solar screens** reduce heat gain by 60-70% (much cheaper than window replacement)
- **Low-E window film** reduces heat gain by 40-50% ($6-12/sqft installed)
- **Light-colored blinds or curtains** on south and west windows
- Close blinds during peak sun hours (10am-4pm)
- Consider awnings over west-facing windows

## System Sizing

Oversized systems are common in South Florida and waste energy.

**Signs your system is oversized:**
- Cools the house quickly but feels clammy/humid
- Short cycles (runs 5-8 minutes, then off for 5-8 minutes)
- Uneven temperatures between rooms

**Why it matters:** An oversized system cools air without running long enough to dehumidify. You end up setting the thermostat lower to feel comfortable, wasting energy.

**Right-sizing:** A proper Manual J load calculation considers your home's square footage, insulation, windows, orientation, and occupancy. Most 1,500-2,000 sqft South Florida homes need 3-4 tons of cooling.

## SEER Ratings

SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) measures cooling efficiency. Higher = more efficient.

| SEER | Monthly Cost (est.) | Annual Savings vs 14 SEER |
|------|--------------------|----|
| 14 | $220 | — |
| 16 | $193 | $324/year |
| 18 | $171 | $588/year |
| 20 | $154 | $792/year |

*Estimates based on 2,000 sqft home, $0.13/kWh, typical South Florida usage.*

Upgrading from a 10 SEER system (common in homes built before 2006) to a 16 SEER system can save $800-1,200/year in South Florida.

## Quick Wins

These cost little or nothing and make an immediate difference:

1. **Close blinds** on sun-facing windows during the day
2. **Run ceiling fans** counterclockwise (allows setting thermostat 2-4F higher)
3. **Don't use the oven** during peak heat — use microwave, air fryer, or grill
4. **Run dishwasher and dryer at night** (they add heat and humidity)
5. **Keep condenser clean** — dirty coils reduce efficiency by 10-30%
6. **Check for duct leaks** — hold a lit incense stick near duct joints and watch for movement
7. **Shade your condenser** — a shaded outdoor unit runs up to 10% more efficiently (but maintain 2ft clearance for airflow)
