# Troubleshooting: High Electric Bills

Your electric bill spiked and AC is the prime suspect. Here's how to investigate.

## Step 1: Confirm It's the AC

Compare your bill to the same month last year. South Florida electricity costs fluctuate with:
- Rate increases from FPL/your utility
- Hotter-than-average months
- Changes in your home (new appliances, more occupants)

**Rule of thumb:** AC accounts for 50-70% of a South Florida electric bill in summer. If your total bill went up 30%+, the AC is likely involved.

## Step 2: Check the Easy Stuff

- **Thermostat setting:** Each degree below 78F adds 3-5% to cooling costs
- **Fan set to ON instead of AUTO:** Runs blower 24/7 ($30-50/month)
- **Dirty air filter:** 5-15% efficiency loss
- **Dirty condenser coils:** 10-30% efficiency loss
- **Vents closed or blocked:** Forces system to work harder
- **Someone changed the thermostat:** Check if a family member set it to 68F

## Step 3: Check for System Problems

### Refrigerant Leak
Low refrigerant forces the compressor to run longer. The system works harder to achieve less cooling.
- **Signs:** Longer run times, reduced cooling, ice on lines
- **Action:** Call a pro for a refrigerant check

### Duct Leaks
Losing 20-30% of cooled air through leaky ducts is like paying to cool your attic.
- **Test:** With system running, feel for air around duct joints in the attic
- **Action:** Professional duct sealing

### Aging System
AC efficiency degrades over time. A 15-year-old system may be running at 60-70% of its original efficiency.
- **Signs:** Gradually increasing bills year over year
- **Action:** Compare your system's age and SEER rating to current standards

### Short Cycling
System turns on and off rapidly (every 5-10 minutes). Each startup uses a surge of energy.
- **Causes:** Oversized system, dirty filter, refrigerant issue, thermostat problem
- **Action:** Replace filter first, then call a pro if cycling continues

## Step 4: Compare Usage

Contact your utility company for:
- Daily kWh usage (most have online portals with graphs)
- Compare weekdays vs weekends (away vs home patterns)
- Compare current year to previous year, same months

Many utilities also offer free energy audits.

## Cost Reduction Checklist

Quick actions that can reduce your bill this month:

- [ ] Set thermostat to 78F when home, 82-85F when away
- [ ] Switch fan from ON to AUTO
- [ ] Replace air filter
- [ ] Clean condenser coils
- [ ] Close blinds on sun-facing windows
- [ ] Run ceiling fans (allows thermostat 2-4F higher)
- [ ] Check for duct leaks
- [ ] Ensure all vents are open and unblocked
