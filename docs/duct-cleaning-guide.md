# When and Why to Clean Your Air Ducts

Your ductwork is the circulatory system of your home. Every cubic foot of air you breathe passes through those ducts multiple times per day. In South Florida, where AC systems run 8-12 months per year, duct condition has an outsized impact on air quality, system efficiency, and energy costs.

## What's Actually in Your Ducts

After years of operation, typical South Florida ductwork accumulates:

- **Dust and particulates** — skin cells, fabric fibers, insulation particles
- **Mold and mildew** — Florida's humidity creates ideal conditions inside dark, cool ductwork
- **Pollen and allergens** — drawn in through return vents and minor duct leaks
- **Pest debris** — insect fragments, rodent droppings (common in attic ductwork)
- **Construction debris** — drywall dust, sawdust, and paint chips from renovations

A 2,000 sq ft home generates about 40 pounds of dust per year. Your air filter catches most of it, but some bypasses the filter through gaps, settles in ductwork, and accumulates over time.

## Signs Your Ducts Need Cleaning

### Visible Indicators

- **Dust puffing from vents** when the system turns on
- **Visible mold** on vent registers or inside duct openings (shine a flashlight inside)
- **Dark streaks** on walls or ceilings around vent openings
- **Debris falling from ceiling vents** — common in homes with flex duct in the attic

### Health-Related Signs

- **Allergy symptoms worsen indoors** — sneezing, congestion, itchy eyes when the AC runs
- **Musty or stale odor** from vents — usually indicates mold or mildew in the ductwork
- **Respiratory issues** that improve when you leave the house
- **Persistent dust** on surfaces despite regular cleaning

### System Performance Signs

- **Uneven airflow** — some rooms get strong airflow, others barely register
- **Higher energy bills** — restricted ducts force the system to work harder
- **System running longer** to reach thermostat set point
- **Weak airflow from registers** even with a clean filter

## The Duct Cleaning Process

Professional duct cleaning uses specialized equipment that homeowners don't have access to. Here's what a proper job involves:

### 1. Inspection

A technician inspects the ductwork with a camera to assess contamination levels, identify damage (tears, disconnections, collapsed sections), and plan the cleaning approach.

### 2. Source Removal

The industry standard is **source removal cleaning**, which physically dislodges and extracts contaminants:

- **Agitation devices** — rotating brushes, air whips, or compressed air nozzles loosen debris from duct walls
- **Negative pressure** — a large vacuum (typically truck-mounted) creates negative pressure in the duct system, pulling dislodged debris toward the collection point
- **Section-by-section** — each supply and return branch is cleaned individually

### 3. Register and Grille Cleaning

All vent registers and grilles are removed, cleaned (or replaced if damaged), and reinstalled.

### 4. Air Handler Cleaning

A thorough job includes cleaning the blower assembly, evaporator coil housing, and drain pan — these components sit upstream of the ductwork and recontaminate clean ducts if left dirty.

### 5. Optional Treatments

- **Antimicrobial treatment** — applied to duct interiors to prevent mold regrowth (especially relevant in South Florida)
- **Duct sealing** — aerosol-based sealant (like Aeroseal) applied from inside the ductwork to seal small leaks

## How Often to Clean Ducts in South Florida

The National Air Duct Cleaners Association (NADCA) recommends cleaning every 3-5 years. In South Florida, the higher end is more realistic due to:

- Year-round system operation (more air passes through the ducts)
- High humidity accelerates biological growth
- Pollen seasons that overlap with peak AC usage
- Sandy soil that infiltrates homes more readily

### Clean More Frequently If

- Anyone in the household has asthma or severe allergies
- You have pets (pet dander accumulates in ductwork)
- You've had water damage or flooding
- You've completed a renovation (construction dust is persistent)
- You notice mold in the home despite regular cleaning
- The home was vacant for an extended period with the AC off

## Duct Cleaning vs. Duct Replacement

Sometimes cleaning isn't enough. Duct replacement may be the better investment if:

| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Flex duct older than 15 years with visible deterioration | Replace |
| Multiple disconnected or collapsed sections | Replace |
| Extensive mold growth that reappears after cleaning | Replace (and identify moisture source) |
| Ducts in unconditioned attic with no insulation | Replace with insulated duct |
| Asbestos-containing duct insulation | Professional abatement and replacement |
| Ductwork properly sized and in good physical condition | Clean |

## What Duct Cleaning Costs

### South Florida Pricing (2026)

- **Basic cleaning** (supply + return, standard home): $400-$700
- **Comprehensive cleaning** (includes air handler, coil, antimicrobial): $700-$1,200
- **Large home or complex systems** (zoned, multi-story): $1,000-$1,800
- **Duct sealing** (Aeroseal or similar): $1,500-$3,000 additional

### Red Flags in Pricing

- **"Whole house for $99"** — this is a bait-and-switch. A real cleaning takes 3-5 hours with a 2-3 person crew and a truck-mounted vacuum. It costs hundreds, not tens.
- **Per-vent pricing without a total estimate** — some companies quote "$25 per vent" then add hidden fees
- **No inspection before quoting** — a legitimate company wants to see the ductwork before committing to a price

## DIY Maintenance Between Professional Cleanings

You can't replicate professional duct cleaning at home, but you can slow contamination:

1. **Change your air filter every 30-60 days** — a clogged filter means more particles bypass into the ductwork
2. **Keep vents open and unobstructed** — closed vents change pressure and can pull contaminants through duct joints
3. **Vacuum vent registers monthly** — use a brush attachment to clean the grille and the first few inches of duct
4. **Maintain indoor humidity below 55%** — mold can't grow without moisture
5. **Run the AC at least 15 minutes daily** even when the home is vacant — stagnant air in dark ducts promotes biological growth

## Choosing a Duct Cleaning Company

### Must-Haves

- **NADCA certification** — the industry standard for duct cleaning professionals
- **Truck-mounted vacuum equipment** — portable units lack the power for proper source removal
- **Licensed and insured** — Florida requires a mechanical contractor license for duct work
- **Written scope of work** — what's included, what's not, and the total price before work begins
- **Before and after photos** — reputable companies document their work

### Questions to Ask

1. What method do you use? (Source removal is the standard)
2. Do you clean the air handler and evaporator coil?
3. How long will the job take? (Under 2 hours for a standard home is a red flag)
4. Do you offer antimicrobial treatment?
5. Can I see your NADCA certification?

## The Connection to System Efficiency

Dirty ductwork doesn't just affect air quality — it costs you money. Contaminant buildup restricts airflow, forcing your system to run longer and harder. Duct leaks (which a cleaning appointment can identify) waste 20-30% of conditioned air into your attic or walls. Between the restricted airflow and the leakage, dirty and leaky ducts can add $300-$500 per year to your energy bills in South Florida.
