# Seasonal HVAC Maintenance for South Florida

South Florida doesn't have four traditional seasons — we have "hot" and "slightly less hot." But your AC system's needs do change throughout the year. Here's what to do and when.

## January - February: The Cool Months

South Florida's coolest months. Your AC runs less, which is the perfect time for maintenance.

**Tasks:**
- Schedule your annual professional tune-up (technicians are less busy)
- Replace thermostat batteries
- Test your heat pump's heating mode (you'll need it for those rare cold snaps)
- Inspect ductwork for visible damage or disconnections
- Check attic insulation — this is the best time to add more if needed

**Why now:** Lower demand means faster scheduling and sometimes lower service rates. Getting ahead of summer ensures your system is ready when temperatures climb.

## March - April: Pre-Summer Prep

Temperatures start climbing. This is your last window before summer strain begins.

**Tasks:**
- Replace air filter (or clean if reusable)
- Clean outdoor condenser unit — remove leaves, pollen, and debris
- Flush condensate drain line with 1 cup white vinegar
- Check condensate drain pan for cracks or rust
- Test system by running for 30 minutes and checking airflow at each vent
- Verify thermostat is set to "Auto" fan mode (not "On")
- Clear vegetation around outdoor unit — maintain 24" clearance on all sides
- Inspect refrigerant lines for damaged insulation

**Pro tip:** If your system struggled last summer, don't wait. Book a professional inspection now.

## May: System Check

The heat is here. Your system should already be prepped.

**Tasks:**
- Confirm system is cooling properly — indoor temp should be 15-20F below outdoor temp
- Check for unusual noises or vibrations
- Verify condensate drain is flowing freely
- Clean or replace air filter if it's been 30 days

## June: Hurricane Season Starts

Hurricane season officially begins June 1. Prepare your HVAC system.

**Tasks:**
- Document your system's make, model, and serial numbers (photo the data plate)
- Know where your electrical disconnect is located
- Check that condenser mounting bolts are tight
- Trim any trees or branches that could fall on your outdoor unit
- If in a flood zone: consider an elevated condenser pad (18"+ above grade)
- Review your homeowner's insurance HVAC coverage

**See also:** [Hurricane Prep Guide](hurricane-prep.md)

## July - August: Peak Heat

The hardest months on your AC. Focus on keeping it running efficiently.

**Tasks:**
- Replace air filter monthly (high pollen + dust = faster clogging)
- Clean condenser coils monthly (rinse with garden hose, top to bottom)
- Check condensate drain weekly during peak humidity
- Keep blinds/curtains closed on sun-facing windows
- Avoid setting thermostat below 72F — it won't cool faster and strains the compressor

**Warning signs during peak heat:**
- System runs for more than 20 minutes without cycling off
- Ice forming on refrigerant lines
- Warm air from vents despite cool thermostat setting
- Water spots on ceiling near air handler

## September - October: Late Hurricane Season

Peak hurricane activity. Stay prepared.

**Tasks:**
- Continue monthly filter changes
- Recheck hurricane prep items
- If system has been running hard all summer, listen for new noises (failing bearings, compressor strain)
- Clean drain pan and check float switch operation

## November - December: Cooldown

The system gets a partial rest. Good time for end-of-year maintenance.

**Tasks:**
- Clean condenser coils one final time
- Flush condensate drain line
- Check and clean return air vents and supply registers
- Consider upgrading your thermostat to a programmable or smart model
- If your system is 10+ years old, get a professional efficiency assessment
- Check ductwork connections — settling and vibration can loosen joints over time
