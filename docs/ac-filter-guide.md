# How to Choose the Right AC Filter

Your AC filter is the single most important -- and most neglected -- component in your HVAC system.

## Filter Sizes
| Size | Common Location |
|------|----------------|
| 16x25x1 | Most common return grille |
| 20x20x1 | Hallway/ceiling return |
| 20x25x1 | Larger cabinets, newer homes |
| 14x20x1 | Smaller returns, apartments |

Note: Nominal vs actual differs. A "20x20x1" filter is actually 19.5 x 19.5 x 0.75 inches.

## MERV Ratings

**MERV 8 -- Best for 80% of homes.** Catches pollen, dust mites, mold spores, pet dander. Change monthly in summer. Good balance of filtration and airflow.

**When to go higher (MERV 11+):** Doctor-diagnosed allergies, asthma, system installed after 2015, or 4-inch media cabinet. Never install MERV 13+ on standard 1-inch slots.

**When to stay at MERV 6:** Systems from before 2000, window units, or known airflow problems.

## South Florida Schedule
- **Summer (May-Oct): Monthly** -- AC runs 12-18 hrs/day
- **Winter (Nov-Apr): Every 2 months** -- or monthly with pets

## What NOT to Do
1. Don't buy MERV 1 fiberglass (catches almost nothing)
2. Don't install MERV 13+ on 1-inch slots (restricts airflow)
3. Don't wash disposable filters (collapses media)
4. Don't run without a filter (coats coil with dust)
5. Don't force wrong-size filter (creates bypass gaps)
