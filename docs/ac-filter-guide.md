# How to Choose the Right AC Filter

Your AC filter is the single most important — and most neglected — component in your HVAC system. A $5–15 filter changed on schedule prevents $5,000+ repairs from frozen evaporator coils, failed compressors, and duct contamination. This guide explains exactly what to buy and why.

## Understanding Filter Sizes

Filter sizes are printed on the cardboard frame (not the mesh). Common sizes in South Florida homes:

| Size (inches) | Common Location |
|---------------|-----------------|
| 16x25x1 | Most common — return air grille or filter cabinet |
| 20x20x1 | Return grille in hallway or ceiling |
| 20x25x1 | Larger filter cabinets, newer homes |
| 14x20x1 | Smaller returns, apartment units |
| 24x24x1 | Commercial or large residential |
| 12x12x1 | Mini-splits, small return grilles |

> ⚠ **Nominal vs actual size**: A "20x20x1" filter actually measures 19.5" × 19.5" × 0.75". Always buy by the nominal size printed on the frame. Measure the slot, not the old filter frame.

### Thickness Matters

- **1-inch filters**: Standard for most residential systems. Best for monthly replacement.
- **2-inch filters**: Found on newer systems with deeper filter cabinets. Lower airflow restriction.
- **4-inch / 5-inch media cabinets**: High-capacity filters changed every 6–12 months. Excellent filtration with less pressure drop, but only if your system was designed for them.

Never install a thicker filter than the cabinet was designed for — it won't seal properly and unfiltered air will bypass the filter entirely.

## MERV Ratings — What You Actually Need

See the [MERV rating guide in our filter tracker tool](../tools/filter-tracker.py) for the full breakdown, but here are the key decisions:

### MERV 8 — The Sweet Spot for South Florida

MERV 8 is the right choice for 80% of homes. It captures:
- Pollen (fine enough for most allergies)
- Dust mites and their debris
- Mold spores
- Pet dander
- 3–10 micron particles

MERV 8 filters are widely available, affordable ($8–15 each), and cause minimal airflow restriction. When changed monthly in summer, they keep coils clean without straining the blower motor.

### When to Go Higher (MERV 11+)

Consider MERV 11 only if you have:
- Doctor-diagnosed moderate to severe allergies
- Asthma triggered by airborne particulates
- A system installed after 2015 (stronger blowers handle the pressure drop)
- A 4-inch or 5-inch media cabinet (much more surface area = less restriction)

**Never install MERV 13 or higher on a standard 1-inch filter slot** unless your HVAC manual explicitly allows it. The airflow restriction will:
- Reduce cooling capacity by 15–30%
- Increase energy bills
- Cause the evaporator coil to freeze (leading to compressor damage)
- Shorten blower motor life

### When to Stay at MERV 6

MERV 6 (basic pleated or fiberglass) is appropriate for:
- Systems installed before 2000 with undersized ductwork
- Window AC units or through-wall units
- Systems with known airflow problems (weak vents, high static pressure)
- Temporary use if MERV 8 filters are unavailable

MERV 6 still catches the large particles that damage equipment. It's better than a dirty MERV 8.

## Filter Types — What's on the Shelf

### Disposable Pleated (Recommended)

The standard for modern systems. Polyester/cotton blend media with a cardboard frame.

- **Cost**: $5–15 each
- **Lifespan**: 1 month (summer), 2–3 months (winter)
- **Best for**: Most systems, MERV 8
- **Pros**: Good filtration, easy to find, cheap enough to change monthly
- **Cons**: Higher airflow resistance than fiberglass

### Fiberglass (Basic Protection)

The original AC filter — a spun glass mesh in a cardboard frame.

- **Cost**: $1–3 each
- **Lifespan**: 1 month (they look dirty fast)
- **MERV**: 1–4 (too low for good IAQ)
- **Best for**: Temporary use only, or window units
- **Cons**: Minimal particle capture — fine dust passes through and coats your coil

### Washable / Reusable

A plastic frame with a foam or mesh media that you rinse and reuse.

- **Cost**: $15–30 one-time
- **Lifespan**: 6–12 months of reuse (wash every 2–4 weeks)
- **MERV**: 4–6 (typically low)
- **Pros**: Zero ongoing cost, eco-friendly
- **Cons**: Must be bone-dry before reinserting (mold risk in humid South Florida). Lower filtration than pleated. Easy to forget to wash.

### High-Capacity (4-inch / Media Cabinet)

Deep pleated filters that fit special cabinets or filter racks.

- **Cost**: $20–40 each
- **Lifespan**: 6–12 months
- **MERV**: 8–16 available
- **Best for**: Systems with a 4" or 5" filter cabinet
- **Pros**: Low airflow restriction due to large surface area, excellent filtration
- **Cons**: Not compatible with standard 1" slots

## The South Florida Replacement Schedule

South Florida's unique climate demands a more aggressive filter schedule:

### Summer (May–October) — Monthly
High humidity + AC running 12–18 hours/day = filters load fast. Change **every 30 days** without exception. A filter that looked clean on day 20 can be fully loaded by day 35 in peak humidity.

### Winter (November–April) — Every 2 Months
AC runs less, but the system still circulates air. A 2-month interval is sufficient for most homes. If you have pets or live near construction, stick to monthly year-round.

### Warning Signs Between Changes

Check your filter immediately if you notice:
- Vents blowing noticeably less air
- AC running longer to reach temperature
- Ice forming on refrigerant lines at the outdoor unit
- Higher electric bill despite similar usage
- Dust accumulating on furniture within days of dusting

## What NOT to Do

1. **Don't buy the cheapest filter** — fiberglass at MERV 1 catches almost nothing and your coil pays the price
2. **Don't buy the most expensive filter** — MERV 13 on a standard system restricts airflow and can freeze your coil
3. **Don't use "electrostatic" or "antibacterial" claims as a substitute for MERV rating** — marketing, not engineering
4. **Don't try to wash a disposable filter** — it collapses the media and ruins the filtration
5. **Don't run without a filter** even for "just a few hours" — dust will coat the evaporator coil and blower wheel, requiring professional cleaning
6. **Don't push a filter into the slot** — if it doesn't slide in easily, you have the wrong size. Forcing it bends the frame and creates gaps
