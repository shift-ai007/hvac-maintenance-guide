# Understanding Your AC Warranty: A Guide for Florida Homeowners

Your AC system is one of the most expensive appliances in your home — a full replacement runs $5,000 to $12,000 in South Florida depending on system size and efficiency tier. Understanding what your warranty covers (and doesn't) can save you thousands. Here's what every Florida homeowner should know.

## Types of AC Warranties

### Manufacturer's Equipment Warranty
This covers defects in the equipment itself — the compressor, coils, and internal components. Standard terms in the US market:

- **Compressor**: 10 years (all major brands — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Goodman, Rheem)
- **Parts**: 5-10 years depending on brand and model tier
- **Labor**: $0 — manufacturers don't cover labor. That's what labor warranties or service plans are for

**Critical Florida detail**: Many manufacturers reduce coverage to 5 years if the unit isn't registered within 60-90 days of installation. If you bought a home with an existing system, check if the original owner registered it. We've seen homeowners quoted $2,800 for a compressor replacement that would have been covered under the original 10-year warranty if registered.

### Labor Warranty (Installer)
The installing contractor typically offers 1-5 years of labor coverage:

- **Standard**: 1 year labor on repairs, 1-2 years on new installations
- **Premium**: Some Florida contractors offer 5-10 year labor warranties on higher-efficiency systems
- **Transferability**: Most labor warranties are non-transferable if you sell the home

### Extended Warranty / Service Plan
These are optional plans you purchase separately, usually costing $200-600/year:

- **Home warranty plans** (AHS, Choice, First American, etc.): Cover AC breakdowns with a service call fee ($75-150 per visit). These are NOT the same as manufacturer warranties — they're insurance policies with coverage limits, exclusions, and fine print
- **HVAC-specific plans**: Some manufacturers offer extended coverage through authorized dealers

## What Florida Homeowners Need to Know

### Salt Air Corrosion — The #1 Warranty Gotcha

Florida's coastal environment accelerates corrosion. Most manufacturer warranties explicitly exclude:
- "Corrosion due to environmental conditions"
- "Damage from salt-laden atmospheres"
- "Rust or deterioration of coils"

This means if your 6-year-old system's condenser coil develops a leak from salt-air pitting (extremely common within 10 miles of the Florida coast), the replacement coil — parts and labor — is on you, typically $1,800-3,200.

Some manufacturers offer "coastal protection" upgrades or extended coil warranties for an additional cost. If you're within 5 miles of the coast, this is worth every penny.

### Registration Requirements

This catches more Florida homeowners than any other warranty issue:

- **Carrier/Bryant/Payne**: Must register within 90 days of installation
- **Trane/American Standard**: Within 60 days
- **Lennox**: Within 60 days
- **Goodman/Amana**: Within 60 days (Amana offers a lifetime compressor replacement if registered)

Without registration, the standard 10-year compressor warranty drops to 5 years. We handle repairs for homeowners in Pembroke Pines, Weston, and Coral Springs who discovered this the hard way — [our AC repair team](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-repair/) sees this regularly.

### Regular Maintenance Requirements

All manufacturers require proof of annual professional maintenance to honor warranty claims. If a compressor fails and you can't show maintenance records, the claim can be denied.

Florida's year-round cooling season makes this especially important — your system runs 2-3x more hours annually than a system in the Midwest. [Professional AC maintenance](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-maintenance/) every 6 months (spring and fall) is the recommended cadence here, not annually.

## When a Warranty Claim Makes Sense vs. Replacement

| Scenario | Warranty Claim | Replace Instead |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Compressor fails, under 8 years | Yes — parts covered | Only if system is R-22 |
| Coil leak, under 6 years | Yes — if registered | Consider if no coastal protection |
| Fan motor, any age | Yes — inexpensive part | No |
| Multiple component failures | No | Yes — indicates systemic issues |
| Compressor fails, 10+ years | No — out of warranty | Yes |
| R-22 refrigerant system | No — gas costs $100-200/lb | Yes — [new installation](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-installation/) |

## Practical Tips

1. **Register your system immediately** — within 30 days of installation. Set a calendar reminder
2. **Keep all maintenance receipts** — digital photos work. Your contractor should provide a detailed invoice listing what was checked
3. **Document serial numbers** — take a photo of the data plate on your outdoor unit
4. **Read the fine print on coastal exclusions** — if you're within 10 miles of the Atlantic, ask about upgraded coil protection
5. **Don't assume a home warranty covers everything** — read the coverage limits on AC repairs carefully
6. **Transfer warranties when buying a home** — request documentation from the seller during closing

The bottom line: a warranty is only as good as your documentation and compliance with maintenance requirements. In Florida's climate, where systems work harder and environmental factors accelerate wear, staying on top of registration and maintenance isn't optional — it's how the warranty actually pays out when you need it.
