# Troubleshooting: AC Not Cooling

Your AC is running but the house isn't getting cool. Walk through this flowchart.

## Step 1: Check the Thermostat
- Is it set to COOL (not HEAT or OFF)?
- Is the set temperature below the current room temperature?
- Are the batteries fresh?
- Is the fan set to AUTO?

**If thermostat is correct → Step 2**

## Step 2: Check the Air Filter
- When was it last changed?
- Pull it out — is it visibly dirty or clogged?
- A severely clogged filter can freeze the evaporator coil

**Action:** Replace the filter. If ice is visible on the indoor unit, turn system OFF, fan to ON, wait 2-4 hours to defrost, then restart with new filter.

**If filter is clean → Step 3**

## Step 3: Check the Outdoor Unit
- Is the condenser fan spinning?
- Is the unit making normal humming sounds?
- Is there debris blocking the coils?
- Is the electrical disconnect turned ON?

**If fan is NOT spinning:**
- Check the breaker at the main panel
- Check the electrical disconnect
- If both are on but fan won't spin: likely a capacitor or motor failure → Call a pro

**If fan IS spinning → Step 4**

## Step 4: Check Airflow at Vents
- Hold your hand near supply vents — is air blowing?
- Is the air cool, lukewarm, or warm?

**If no airflow:** Blower motor may have failed → Call a pro

**If airflow is warm:**
- Check if outdoor unit is running (both fan AND compressor)
- If compressor isn't running (outdoor fan spins but no humming/vibration): likely compressor or capacitor issue → Call a pro
- If compressor IS running but air is warm: likely low refrigerant → Call a pro

## Step 5: Check for Ice
- Look at the refrigerant lines going into the indoor unit
- Look at the evaporator coil (if accessible)
- Any frost or ice visible?

**If iced up:**
1. Turn system OFF
2. Set fan to ON
3. Wait 2-4 hours
4. Replace air filter
5. Restart system
6. If it ices up again within 24 hours → Call a pro (refrigerant leak likely)

## Common Causes Summary

| Symptom | Likely Cause | DIY? |
|---------|-------------|------|
| No power to unit | Tripped breaker, bad disconnect | Check breaker |
| Fan runs, no cooling | Low refrigerant, bad compressor | Call a pro |
| Weak airflow | Dirty filter, blower issue | Replace filter |
| Ice on coils | Low refrigerant, airflow restriction | Defrost, then call a pro |
| Short cycling | Oversized system, dirty filter, low refrigerant | Check filter, then call a pro |
